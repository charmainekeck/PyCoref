
              
 Business Brief:
Western Union Corp.
         
                        
          


 Western Union Corp. said its subsidiary, Western Union Telegraph Co., agreed to extend labor contracts with the United Telegraph Workers and the Communications Workers of America for three years. 
 The contracts, signed in 1985 following strikes by the unions, were to expire Aug. 6, according to the Upper Saddle River, N.J.-based communications systems company. 
 The agreement provides for a one-year extension of the existing contracts. 
 At the end of the first and second years, the company or the unions may choose to renegotiate wages, pensions and health care. 
 The accord also provides for cost-of-living adjustments in the second and third years. 
 The United Telegraph Workers union represents 4,400 Western Union employees around the country. 
 The Communications Workers of America represents 300 company employees in New York City.
